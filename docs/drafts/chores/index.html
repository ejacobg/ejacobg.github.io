Chores

https://omnivore.app/me/turning-music-into-a-chore-is-how-i-became-a-musician-the-scapeg-18a2e05c22c https://the.scapegoat.dev/turning-music-into-a-chore-is-what-made-me-an-artist

Applying to jobs and interviewing has been particularly hard for me. For one thing, I find it difficult and somewhat embarrassing to write and talk about myself. Applying is inherently making yourself vulnerable. I forget facts about my own self and what I’ve accomplished, and that forgetfulness can lead me to believe that I haven’t done anything at all with my life. This is exaggerating of course, but if I really try hard to remember, my accomplishments don’t seem as glamorous as I would have hoped.

The “music as chores” article feels like one of the better perspectives on my work. I’m not sure exactly why though. It seems like associating my work with “chores”–something with a negative connotation–would make me less likely to want to work on them. In any case, I do feel productive, even if most of this time hasn’t actually been tracked lately.
